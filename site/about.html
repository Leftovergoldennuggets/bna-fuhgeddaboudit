About This Project

Self-driving cars are now a daily reality on American streets. Waymo operates a commercial robotaxi service in San Francisco, Phoenix, Los Angeles, Austin, and Atlanta — the largest autonomous vehicle deployment in the world.

But how safe are these cars, really? This project gathers every publicly reported crash involving a Waymo vehicle and presents the data in a way anyone can understand.

Why This Matters

Every time a Waymo vehicle is involved in a reportable crash — one resulting in injury, airbag deployment, or property damage exceeding $1,000 — the company is legally required to report it to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration. That data is public, but it is buried in government spreadsheets that are difficult to parse and full of technical jargon.

We built this website because we believe the public deserves to see this data clearly. Autonomous vehicles are being tested on the streets where people live, work, and walk their kids to school. Residents in these cities did not sign up for a safety experiment, but they are part of one.

Our goal is not to argue that self-driving cars are safe or unsafe. It is to show you the evidence and let you draw your own conclusions.

Design Principles

  • Transparency first. Every number on this site can be traced back to a specific public data source. Our full methodology is documented. Our code is open-source.
  • Plain language. We translate technical crash classifications into everyday English. If your neighbor would not understand a label, we rewrite it.
  • Let the data speak. We avoid editorializing. We present the numbers, the context, and the primary sources. You decide what they mean.
  • Evergreen. This site updates itself. When new crash reports are published, our pipeline pulls the latest data and regenerates every chart and statistic automatically.

Data Sources

This project draws on two primary public data sources:

  • NHTSA Standing General Order reports — The federal government requires all companies testing Level 3+ autonomous vehicles to report crashes meeting specific thresholds (injury, airbag deployment, or significant property damage). This dataset includes incident narratives, severity assessments, and location information.
  • Waymo Safety Impact Data Hub — Waymo publishes its own curated dataset that adds crash type classifications and links each incident to the corresponding federal report.
  • Waymo mileage milestones — A dataset of Waymo's cumulative rider-only miles over time, compiled by Anders Eidesvik from press releases, public statements, and archived web data.

We merge these two sources in an automated pipeline that runs quarterly. The full technical details are on our methodology page.
